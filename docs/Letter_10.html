Stockholm, May 16, 1935

Dear Consul General:

I understand that Miss Thoreson has written
to you that it was my fault that Mr. Bendix refused to take the
statue Miss Thoreson sent to America. She is supposed to have
said that I wanted too “big a commission". There was no question
of a commission, nor did I have anything to do with it(the refu-
sal). The matter was entirely in the hands of Dr. J. F. Lewis, and
even if Mr. Bendix had taken the statue I would not have received


I spent over $200.00 of my own money trying to
straighten out the matter and to be accused of trying to get com-
mission under such conditions makes me quite disgusted.

Unless Miss Thoreson retracts her statements
writing to you and sends me something is going to
happen. Unless I have such a certified copy in my hands in Phila-
delphia by July 1st next, I shall order Miss Thoreson to remove the
statue from the Museum and unless she removes it by August 1st I
shall have it sent to storage, where she will have to pay storage
at so much a month (about $10.00 monthly).

I have had some hope of selling it through a
friend of mine (whom you know), but I shall now drop all negotiat-
ions.

If and when she sends you the letter of re-
traction I am sure you will out of fairness give it the same
publicity as you gave the original statement.

I am sorry I found no opportunity of seeing you
again before I leave, but I am on my way to Gothenburg tomorrow
and there are so many things I must do before I leave.

With very best wishes and greetings I remain,

Sincerely yours,

Amandus Johnson

Copy to Miss Thoreson and Minister Boström.
From the enclosed statement from my former secretary you will see
how the matter really stood. I shall have a certified statement f
her when I return to America, which will prove that the above state-
ments are correct and that I had nothing to do with the refusal of
Mr. Bendix Please return the statement of my secretary, as I want
to send it to Minister Boström.
