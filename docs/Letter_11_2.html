, May 21st, 1935

OHL/UR

Dr. Amandus Johnson,
John Morton Memorial Museum,
Philadelphia.
Pennsylvania


My dear Dr. Johnson,

Your letter of the 16th of May came to hand as I re-
turned from a few days trip south and I am very sorry, indeed
that I had no chance to meet you again and to continue the
pleasant discussion we had when you were kind enough to visit
me in my office.

I am also sorry that we did not meet se that I could
clear up the misunderstanding, on which your letter to me
seems to be based. I tried to get hold of you over the te-
lephone both in Stockholm and Gothenburg, but did not succeed.

Before I had an opportunity to write my answer I re-
ceived a call from Miss Thoresen. I may mention that this
was the first time I ever saw Miss Thoresen or had any kind of
communication with her in the above matter. This very charming
lady was rather upset about your letter to me, of which you
have sent her a copy. Since she had not done, what you seem
to think she had, she was not afraid of your threats but very
sorry, that you should suspect her. She told me, that she had
never even had a thought, that you had tried to hold up the
sale of her statue. On the contrary, she felt, that you had
been very kind to her and done all that was possible to be of
assistance to her, even if she was sorry, that you had not
accepted the cheque from Mr. Bendix in the first place.

As for my part, I do not understand, why you got me
mixed up in this matter. I have had nothing to do with it
from beginning to end, received no letter from Miss Thoresen
or anybody else about it and taken no action of any kind. It
would be rather interesting to know, who could be the mischief
maker, who has been telling you differently.

I do not see, why things of this kind have to come
up between you and me. As you are aware, I have sometimes
differred with you in my ideas - and always plainly told you
so - but I have never doubted the disihterested zeal, with

which you have pursued whatever cause you have thought right
and I have always been under the impression, that this respect
for you has been reciprocated.

Instead of writing a letter like that, would it not
have not been a good idea to get in touch with me or with Miss
Thoresen, which would have cleared up the matter without this
unnecessary correspondence. Incidentally would it not be
wise not to believe everything that is told to you, when it
must seem unreasonable, that it is true.

I hope, that if there is anything I can do to assist
you in the work, which we were discussing in our meeting here,
you will let me know,

In the meantime I remain, with kindest regards and
best greetings,

Very sincerely yours.

2022 Charlotte Selander and Sofia Rödström.
