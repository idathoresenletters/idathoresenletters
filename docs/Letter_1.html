Letter from Amandus Johnson to Ida von Schulzenheim, 03 June 1930

Home | Top Layer |

Nineteenth and Pattison Sts.,
Philadelphia, Pa., June 3, 1930

Miss Ida von Schulzenheim,
78 Riddaregatan
Stockholm, Sweden.

Dear Miss von Schulzenheim:

We have made considerable progress with the arrangements of the
exhibit. Last night men from the Academy of Fine Arts worked here until
past ten o’clock and they are here this morning. I think they shall
finish hanging the pictures tomorrow. We have not followed your arrange-
ment at all, as the President of the Academy of Fine Arts thought we
should place them in a manner that would suit American taste and be more
effective in displaying the pictures. The paintings alone fill up nine
rooms, as we have given them plenty of space. We have not as yet put up
the sculptures and the other things. I believe we shall not arrange the
textiles or the embroidery work, as that might fade in the sun, and I do
not believe that we should sell them this summer. Likewise, I believe we
shall keep the small things packed in boxes until the fall. It will be too
difficult to look after the little things when visitors come here. The
collection is insured against all risks, fire, theft, etc.

It seems that someone has made inquiries in New York about the con-
dition of the exhibit and what precautions have been taken against risks
etc. I did not tell the Academy of Fine Arts about this, for if they knew
about those inquiries they would probably not care to have anything further
to do with the exhibit. I would, however, advise you very strongly, not
to make inquiries from any other parties about this exhibit, if you want
us to handle it.

I am sending you a cable today, asking you to come over on the 14th
if you can, to be with us on the 24th, when we have a sort of private
opening. You could stay a week and then go back on a Swedish boat. I
should like this very much, as we then could talk matters over concerning
prices and other points which are not very clear to us. We shall not
print the catalogue until the fall, but use a manuscript copy in the
sale of the Art. We have made no further effort to sell any more of
your paintings and pieces of sculpture and will not until I hear by letter
from you. However, we have had a number of inquiries. For instance, I
have a party who wants to buy "The Windmill" by Akerman, and the Landscape
in Scania; but they have offered only $150.00 for "The Windmill" and
$175.00 for the Landscape. I could not sell it for that as I do not know
if the artist would accept that price, especially since the Academy of
Fine Arts wants 15 %. If however, the artist is willing to accept those
prices, and we cannot get the price given in the catalogue, we shall sell
it to the party in question. Do not cable the artist’s decision on this
as there is time enough to write. I have the name and the address of the
party and can always get in touch with her. It is quite possible that
we get an offer for the full price that this lady will pay the price you
have intimated, although she said she would not.

On account of these conditions, it would be very desirable if you
could come here for about a week or two weeks. We could then have the
exhibit in fine shape in the fall. However, I am myself going to Stockholm
this summer and hope to be there towards the end of July unless my Doctors
should forbid me to travel. Unfortunately I must go to the hospital tomorrow
or the next day for observation, as they do not seem to know what is the
trouble with me. They assure me, however, that I shall be out in about a
week or at the most two weeks and will be on my feet by the time we have our
annual meeting and private opening.

Hoping that it may be possible for you to come here and with very best
wishes to you and your Society, I remain,

Very sincerely yours,

Amandus Johnson.

AJ:N

P.S. I am sorry you did not send the original manuscript with your English
translation as we fail to understand what you mean by your phraseology in
one or two cases, for instance you say “wastecloset", so far as I know there
is no such word in English and there could not be one. Do you mean "Skräp-
rum" or "skräpkammare"? In any event we could not use "wastecloset"-- that
means nothing. Your translation of “Handarbetets Vänner" is also inaccurate
and a great many other terms will not do. However, I shall revise the Eng-
lish but I wish you would send the original Swedish in case you send any
other manuscripts. I am anxiously waiting for the ten pages of History of
Swedish Art which will make the catalogue valuable and make it easy to sell.
Beside I can find no portrait of the Crown Princess nor or the other prin-
ces.
