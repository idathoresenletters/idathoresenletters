Handwritten in upper left corner:

January 06, 1931

Miss Ida von Schulzenheim,
78 Riddaregatan
Stockholm, Sweden.


Dear Miss von Schulzenheim:

Dr. Johnsom has gone to Chicago due to some complications about
the art works. He took the catalogue and photographs of paintings
with him and will try to persuade certain Chicago people to buy
other things and he is also going to Kewanee to see Mr. Peter A.
Waller who is, I believe, well known in Sweden, and he vill probably
buy certain things. Conditions here are really worse than people
imagine, and it is very difficult to do anything.

As soon as Dr. Johnsom returns from his trip I shall place
your recent letter and other matters before him. I have just had a
telegramm from him telling me to cable you the following:

"Due to conditions opening impossible until spring.
Making efforts to sell objects. Letter and check soon".

I have also written to the man interested in the statue "Nymf vid
källan" by Mrs Alice Nordin, and he will undoubtedly send a check
for $100 at once. As soon as it arrives the sum will be forwarded
to you.

Yours very truly


Lillian Nyqvist
